Abstract: THINKPAD BATTERY INFORMATION

General Battery Statement
Extending Battery Life. The amount of battery power consumed by your computer depends on such conditions as the usage frequency operating temperature period in storage (if unused). The following tips can help you to extend the life of your battery:

(All Battery types)
If your battery is brand new and you are charging it for the first time, it might not become fully charged due to the chemical characteristics in the cell. To maximize performance, ensure that all chemicals in the battery are fully activated by cycling the battery (fully charge, then fully discharge) three times. This procedure is also recommended if you stored the battery for a few months.

Battery Charging and Discharging Cycles

  • Do not charge the battery pack until all of its power is used.
  • Once you start charging the battery pack, do not use the system on battery power until it is fully charged. You can use the system on AC power during this time.

Whenever possible, use the ThinkPad battery power-saving modes:

  1. Standby: The LCD and the hard disk drive are turned off.
  2. Suspend: All tasks are stopped and stored in memory to save power.
  3. Hibernation: All tasks are stopped. The memory data and the current status of the computer are stored in the hard disk drive; then the power is turned off.

Decrease the LCD brightness.
Use Advanced Power Management (APM). Each operating system comes with its own APM.
Shut down power to I/O ports if they are not being used.
Remove the battery pack and keep it in a cool place if you will not be using the computer for a long period of time.
If your battery obtains less then 60% of its original charge life it is close to its normal life expectancy and you may want to consider a replacement. For Nickel Metal Hydride Batteries do the following steps before obtaining a new battery.

Nickel Metal Hydride Batteries
The amount of time a ThinkPad will operate on the main Nickel Metal hydride battery is directly tied to the correct conditioning of the battery, the correct system setup, and configuration of system.

If the system diagnostics run error free, you should take the following steps to increase battery power-on-time. Most of this information is contained in the User's Guide or User's Reference.

Initial Use:
To realize full battery potential the main battery will have to be fully charged and discharged from 3 to 6 times after its initial use. The battery will then be conditioned to accept a full charge.

If a Nickel Metal Hydride battery is continually recharged before it is fully discharged it will not stay conditioned. An unconditioned battery will have a shorter discharge cycle. This can occur when a system running on battery for a short period is charged with an AC power supply, docking station or port replicator. For example, if you remove the machine from power for 10 or 15 minutes, you should let the battery continue to discharge before putting the machine back on AC power or a docking station or port replicator.

If improper battery conditioning is suspected the following process will recondition the battery and allow it to accept as full charge as possible . A battery may require reconditioning every two months if the above minimal charge and discharge methods are used. The conditioning process will not adversely affect battery life.

  1. Run the system until it goes into suspend because of low battery power. (The suspend setting may have to be changed, if it is set to suspend on time.)
  2. Leave the system off for about 15 minutes.(Do not charge.)
  3. Turn the system on until it again goes into suspend because of low battery power.
  4. The battery is now fully discharged.
  5. Fully charge the battery.

If a percentage indicator is present, continue to charge the battery for 30 more minutes after it reaches 100%.

If LEDs are used leave the battery charge for 30 minutes after the charge completion indication comes on.

** Repeat the above discharge procedure a minimum of 3 times.
Then follow the normal recharging method in the Quick Reference manual (Charge the battery after it has gone into suspend mode because of a low battery condition.)
Note: The system can be used during the three conditioning discharge and charge cycles.

The above conditioning should increase the capacity of the battery from 80% to 100% of its original charge life capacity. If your battery obtains less then 60% it is close to its normal life expectancy and you may want to consider a replacement.

The following tips will extend the operational discharge time of all types of rechargeable batteries: To adjust power management settings click Start (lower left of screen), click Programs, click ThinkPad, and click ThinkPad Configuration.
Note: Not all ThinkPads have this utility, those systems control power management either by the BIOS or the operating system.

Set the LCD brightness control to the darkest viewing level that is comfortable. A bright screen will consume more power.

Use Power Management to set:

  • Unused ports to off.
  • Hard disk drive off time low.
  • LCD blank time low.
  • CPU speed on low or automatic.
  • Power schemes to match your normal operating times. This will ensure the system suspends if you are not actively using it.
